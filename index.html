My Project

Project Idea:

A system which allows individuals to input in their generalized financial details in relation to a specific area they need assistance with; whether it be their superannuation, or their debt, and provide a general financial plan for them to achieve these goals. This could be inputted by an application, either online via website, or on a smart phone, and allow not only for peace of mind with financial decisions, but also to allow for the individual to improve their financial literacy in a user friendly experience. The app/website would also include educational information around areas which are found to be areas of high use, for example, mortgages.

Motivation:

With the Australian financial industry being put under lots of scrutiny over the past decade, including the Royal Commission investigation and the GFC of 2007, people are very hesitant and untrusting of many financial services. This project idea would eliminate the use of a seemingly ‘broken’ and untrustworthy system, and allow for individuals to make their own educated decisions with their finances. From a marketable perspective, this would give the consumer of the product a format to skip the middle man (i.e., Financial Advisor or Banker), and to produce successful results with their financial goals.

Description:

My application/website will be broken down into 3 specific areas which Australian’s often have issues with financially. A membership educational service will be set up that will give the client access to the three topics with information and potentially articles and blogs from financially literate individuals. It will also allow for the personalized general advice system in each area, where personal details are injected into the system, and a modelling based output would give clients better understanding of how their ideas would realistically impact them.

Areas of interest:

SUPERANNUATION

The super industry in Australia was set up over two decades ago and has now grown to be the largest pool of retirement funds in the world. Although this industry is so large, it is also regarded as the least accessed industry in Australia; i.e., people know their employer is paying super, but have no idea where the funds are invested or how they are invested. My project would increase the awareness of the necessity of one client’s superannuation by outlining how the funds allow for healthy and comfortable retirement, that does not rely solely on the age pension. The application will provide graphs based on statistical trends in the markets, which would show the client how their funds would both grow over time, and feed a pension to them once their income stops.

DEBT:

It is very common for individuals to fall into debt holes at very young ages with money being so easily accessible through credit cards, personal loans and mortgages. What people often overlook, is how their lifestyle will be effected once they must start servicing these debts. My application would allow clients to input their current expenditure of essentials, and lifestyle spending, and model what a reasonable amount of debt would be to minimize the opportunity cost of debt. The saying ‘there is no such thing as healthy debt,’ is not exactly true. Debt allows for people to make the purchases they need to make, whether it be a family home or a holiday, and pay it back at affordable weekly, monthly or yearly rates. The cost of this debt is what people often overlook, but with the educational knowledge derived from my application, individuals would have a stronger understanding of what a reasonable amount of debt would be, and through projectional graphs show how long it would take to reasonably pay off the debt.

Savings:

Personal savings is an area that people often struggle to make headwind with as it is much easier to spend money than earn it. A savings tool in the app would show clients where they would be after a predetermined time frame with savings in either a bank, managed fund, or individual investments. This will also be displayed on projectional models to give clients an easy to read understanding of how their savings plan would play out. This section would also include educational information on different investment options that arise through market trends or new areas of interest that open to the public.

TOOLS:

Initially the project would need a stable platform developed that would allow for the introduction of modelling software for a variety of different outcomes. The platform would start with a website based design that would eventually move to an application base, once the initial testing of the platform has been released to the public. Modelling software could be developed internally or purchased through existing providers. Generalized advice documents would be developed from the system in which we would need to have a platform that can adapt to the different inputs of each client.

Skills:

Initially basic HTML skills would be needed to develop the online based website design. This would need to be expanded to App development areas for the integration of the website and application to be seamless. We would need to have the skills to build the modelling, and can change it to align with the multitude of regulatory reform that occurs within the industry.

Outcome:

If this project is successful, members would have access to a multitude of tools and educational streams which would allow for successful implementation of their financial goals. Although similar systems exist, no system currently provides generalized advice documents, which would expand the existing advice industry to new areas. Being the leading technological developer in this area, we would be the key provider of the advice and create greater financial literacy and comfort for all households in Australia. .
